Easy Buckwheat Pancakes

Vegan, Sugar, Gluten and Dairy free, contains nuts Buckwheat contains no gluten and is good for those who need to avoid wheat.

These dense, filling and nutritious pancakes are high in protein and fibre and are both tasty and satisfying. High in protein, essential fats and dietary fibre. Low GL.

Ingredients

200g buckwheat flour

60ml hot water

300ml unsweetened almond milk

1 tbsp. of almond butter

½ tsp. of viridian maca powder

1 tsp. of viridian organic raw coconut oil

Pinch of Himalayan fine rock salt

3 tablespoons viridian organic raw coconut oil

Method

  1. Place the flour in a large bowl. Add the hot water, almond milk, a pinch of salt, almond butter, maca and 1 tsp. of coconut oil mix thoroughly.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a large frying pan or on a griddle over a medium heat. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the pan and cook both sides until golden brown.

Mediterranean Chickpea Stew

Vegan, Sugar, Gluten, Dairy and Nut Free

Vitamin C is a huge help when it comes to absorbing iron from plant sources. When beans (a good source of iron) are cooked with tomatoes (a good source of vitamin C), the combination can double the absorption of iron. This is easy to prepare and a delight to devour! I’ve made this several times to rave reviews. High in protein, vitamin C, dietary fibre, essential fats and protein. Low GL..

Ingredients

2 medium leeks

2 tablespoons viridian organic raw coconut oil

1½ teaspoons ground coriander

1 teaspoon ground cumin

1 teaspoon ground fennel seed

½ teaspoon Himalayan fine rock salt

⅛ teaspoon cayenne pepper

1 x 400g can of chopped tomatoes with their juices

1 x 400g can of chickpeas, rinsed and drained

12 Kalamata or green olives, pitted and chopped Zest of 1 lemon

1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice

Cooked millet or basmati rice, or wholewheat bread, to serve

Method

  1. Trim the leeks, slit them in half lengthwise and rinse well under running water to remove any sand or grit. Coarsely chop the leeks.
  2. Heat the oil in a large saucepan over a medium heat. Add the leeks, coriander, cumin, fennel seed, salt and cayenne pepper. Cook, stirring often, for about 5 minutes, until the leeks are soft.
  3. Add the tomatoes with their liquid, the chickpeas, olives and lemon zest and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium to low and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 15 minutes, until the tomato sauce is very thick. Remove from the heat and stir in the lemon juice. Serve immediately over cooked millet or rice, or with wholewheat bread.

Rice crackers and aubergine dip

Good source of dietary fibre, B vitamins and potassium. As well as providing a supply of phytonutrients, through aubergine, which have antioxidant properties that protect essential fatty acids necessary for healthy brain functioning.

Ingredients

1 aubergine

1 clove garlic

½ lemon juiced and zest

Small bunch of coriander

1 or 2 rice crackers

Drizzle of Olive Oil

Method

  1. Heat the oven to 200C/Gas 6/400F.
  2. Put the aubergine on a baking sheet and roast in the oven for 30-40 minutes until tender.
  3. Put the aubergine flesh in a food processor with the garlic, lemon juice, zest and coriander.
  4. With the motor still running, pour in olive oil and blend until the mixture is smooth.
  5. Spread on top of rice crackers.

Classic mushroom risotto

High in vitamin D2, beta glucans for immune support and high fibre to retain satiety and keep sugar cravings and bay.

Ingredients

50g dried porcini mushrooms

1 vegan stock cube

2 tablespoons Viridian Nutrition Organic Coconut Oil

1 onion, chopped

2 garlic cloves

250g chestnut mushrooms, chopped

2-3 teaspoons of Herbes de Provence

300g risotto rice

175ml dry white wine

Handful of fresh parsley, chopped

3 tablespoons of nutritional yeast

Viridian Organic Hemp Seed Oil

Method

  1. Soak the dried mushrooms in one litre of boiling water, stand for 20 minutes. Drain and retain the liquid. Remove the rehydrated mushrooms and chop.
  2. Add the stock cube to the mushroom liquid and mix until combined.
  3. Heat the coconut oil in a deep frying pan over a medium flame, sauté the onions and garlic until soft.
  4. Stir in all the mushrooms, season with sea salt, ground black pepper and Herbes de Provence, cook until the mushrooms have soften.
  5. Add the rice to the pan and stir until combined, add the wine and stir until the rice absorbs the fluid.
  6. Add a quarter of the mushroom stock, stirring until absorbed, maintaining a simmer.
  7. Add the same quantity of stock, stirring until absorbed. The rice should become creamy and plump. Continue until all the stock has been absorbed by which time the rice should be ‘ala dente’. If not keep cooking and add further water if necessary.
  8. Take off the heat and stand for 10 minutes with the lid on the pan.
  9. Serve the risotto in bowls topped with a sprinkling of nutritional yeast, drizzle of Organic Hemp Seed Oil and the parsley.
