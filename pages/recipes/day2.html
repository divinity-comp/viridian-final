Fresh garden salad with roasted seed and nut sprinkles

Mixed seeds and nuts are a great source of iron and magnesium, restoring energy and increasing vitality. High source of protein, essential fats, dietary fibre and vitamin A, which is a powerful antioxidant involved in healthy vision and neurological functioning.

Ingredients

75 g mixed nuts and seeds

Bag of mixed seasonal salad & herb leaves

Quarter of a cucumber

5 cherry tomatoes

½ bell pepper

1 spring onion

2-3 radish

Method

  1. Take 75 grams of mixed nuts and seeds, place in a roasting dish and roast in the oven at 200C until golden.
  2. Fill one third of your plate with mixed seasonal salad and herb leaves. Add sliced cucumber, quartered tomatoes, sliced pepper, chopped spring onions, sliced radish.
  3. Sprinkle the roasted nuts and seeds on top

Optional: Whisk Viridian Organic Black Seed Oil with the juice of half a lemon, add some basil and dress the salad.

Coconut crunches

Vegan Sugar, Gluten and Dairy free contains nuts

Perfect for a nibble or with your hot evening drink.

Before you know it, you’ll be acting the nut! High in essential fats, plant protein, dietary fibre, vitamin E and zinc. Low GL.

Ingredients

170g pecans

170g almonds

290g creamed coconut

3 tablespoons of viridian organic raw coconut oil

¼ teaspoon Himalayan fine rock salt

25g desiccated coconut, toasted

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 150°C/gas mark 2/300°F.
  2. Spread the nuts on a baking sheet and toast very lightly for 15 minutes.
  3. Process the agave sugar in a food processor until it’s very fine. Place the jar of creamed coconut in hot water to soften it. In a medium-sized bowl, beat the creamed coconut, coconut oil, agave sugar and salt with an electric mixer until creamy. Fold in the toasted nuts and mix well with a large spoon until all the nuts are well coated.
  4. Line the baking sheet with parchment paper and spread out the nut mixture evenly. Sprinkle with the desiccated coconut and place the baking sheet in the refrigerator.
  5. When cold, break into bite-size pieces. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to ten days.
Dinner

Serves
4

Sweet potato & Quinoa curry

Vegan, Sugar, Gluten, Dairy and Nut free, contains Soya and Celery

When making broth, I usually use organic, low-salt, noyeast, gluten-free stock cubes, which you can find in your local health-food shop. High in plant protein, dietary fibre, essential fats and B vitamins.

Ingredients

2 tablespoons viridian organic raw coconut oil

1 large red onion, finely chopped

1 clove of garlic, minced

1 teaspoon grated fresh root ginger

1 unsalted, gluten-free vegetable stock cube

400ml hot water

2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced

1 x 400g can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed

200g quinoa

1 tablespoon mild curry powder

1 tablespoon ground cumin

80g baby spinach

Himalayan fine rock salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a large pan over a low to medium heat. Add the onion, garlic and ginger and cook for 5–8 minutes, until softened.
  2. Dissolve the stock cube (if using) in 400ml of hot water in a Pyrex jug. Add the broth or water, sweet potatoes, chickpeas, quinoa, curry powder and cumin to the pan.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and cover.
  4. Simmer for 20 minutes, until the quinoa and sweet potatoes are cooked through. Add a little water if the liquid evaporates before the quinoa is fully cooked.
  5. Add the spinach and stir well. Cover again and cook just until the spinach has wilted. Add salt and pepper if desired.
